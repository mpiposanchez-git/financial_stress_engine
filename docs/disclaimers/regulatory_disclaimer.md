# Regulatory Disclaimer

## Educational Simulation Only

This platform is an **educational simulation tool** designed to help UK households understand their potential financial resilience under hypothetical stress scenarios.

**This tool does not provide:**
- Financial advice
- Investment advice
- Product recommendations
- Regulated advice of any kind

---

## FCA Regulation

This tool is **not regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA)** and does not constitute a regulated financial service under the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (FSMA).

The outputs of this tool are **illustrative estimates only**, produced by a simplified computational model. They should not be relied upon as the basis for any financial decision.

If you require financial advice, you should consult a qualified financial adviser who is authorised and regulated by the FCA. You can find an FCA-regulated adviser at [https://www.fca.org.uk/consumers/finding-adviser](https://www.fca.org.uk/consumers/finding-adviser).

---

## UK Context

This tool is designed for use in the context of UK household finances:

- The model is UK-focused, while reporting can be shown in **GBP, EUR, or USD** using user-provided FX assumptions.
- Mortgage assumptions are based on common UK mortgage structures (repayment and interest-only).
- Stress scenarios are illustrative and do not reflect Bank of England stress testing methodology.

---

## Limitations of the Model

Users should be aware of the following limitations:

1. **Simplified cashflow model:** The engine computes a single-period monthly cashflow. It does not model multi-year amortisation paths, tax effects, benefit entitlements, or asset liquidation strategies.

2. **No product-level detail:** The model does not account for specific mortgage product features such as early repayment charges, overpayment allowances, or tracker rate behaviour.

3. **Static assumptions:** The model does not capture dynamic effects such as changing employment markets, property value changes, or evolving interest rate environments beyond the user-specified stress inputs.

4. **Monte Carlo uncertainty bands are not forecasts:** The probabilistic outputs reflect uncertainty in the user's inputs, not actual probability distributions of future macroeconomic outcomes.

5. **No tax modelling:** Income is assumed to be net of tax. The model does not compute tax liabilities or benefit entitlements arising from income changes.

6. **No liability for outcomes:** The operators of this tool accept no liability for any financial decisions made in reliance on the outputs of this simulation.

---

## Who Should Use This Tool

This tool is suitable for:

- Individuals seeking to understand their household's financial resilience in a general, illustrative sense.
- Financial educators and researchers using it as a demonstration tool.
- Households wanting to explore "what if" scenarios in a low-stakes, anonymous environment.

This tool is **not suitable** as a substitute for regulated financial advice, mortgage advice, or debt counselling.
